First question I want to go throug is: What is IPC and RPC, and why it is important in Hardward design flow?

  • IPC or Inter-Process Communication which refers specifically to the mechanisms an operating system provides to allow the processes to manage shared data.
  • RPC or Remote Procedure Call, which builds on some form of inter-process communication, provides a mechanism for calling a procedure in a program running on a remote system is called.

Approaches

There are several ways to achieve the goal. Share the data from one process to another process. This is very common, as you may know, digital ASIC design flow is splitted to several stages, floorplan, placement, cts, postcts, route, postroute, physical verification (drc/lvs) etc. Between each stage, we need to hand over the work from one to another.

Metrics

So for hardware design CAD framework, how could we choose? I just share the priority in my mind. You may have your priority in your organization. You may not need to find the most advanced technology, you should only need to figure out the best-fit solutions. This is the engineering mindset :)

Metric Priority Explanation
customer high It is based on the knowledge of pruduct customers
stability high Re-try or bad data is critical.
adoption high It should be supported by different EDA tools, different program languages.
portability medium Very good to have.
dependency medium Make the dependency as “easy “as possible
deployment medium It must be easy to deploy.
spead low Real time and highly concurrent is not critical.

So based on the table above, I vote the first 3 high priority. In the hardware design scenario, CAD framework is serving, helping a specific domain engnieers, they are good at hardware design, and hardware design has various background knowledge requirement. Some ASIC frontend engineers may like python, perl, and makefile, but some ASIC backend engineers may prefer tcl, perl, shell, or EDA tool specific language. Backend process relies on tool a lot nowadays.

Stability is anohter very important one, the cost of re-run is very high. For software, web service, maybe re-send a request will not affect any user experience and only takes one seconds to re-do something. But, in ASIC Backend flow, after routing is done, if you want to fix the floorplan issue, we may already waste a lot of engineering time, license spense, and pay the business loss of losing market time.

adoption is similar to stability, for ASIC flows, we rely on EDA tools a lot. If the tool can not support the methodology, technology we choose, then communicating with EDA tools will become very bad.

for others, they may good to have or just needed in some scenarios.
